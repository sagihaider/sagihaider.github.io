excerpt: '[IJCNN_2'15] In EEG-based brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), varying statistical properties of signals during session transfers can degrade performance. This study assesses four frequency band selection methods for binary motor imagery classification, including forward-addition, backward-elimination, intersection, and union. Using BCI Competition 2008 dataset 2A, backward-elimination yields the highest improvement, raising average accuracy from 77.06% to 79.09% over the filter bank CSP algorithm.'
