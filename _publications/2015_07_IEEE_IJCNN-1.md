excerpt: '[IJCNN_1'15] Learning in non-stationary environments with dataset shifts, particularly covariate shifts, is challenging. This phenomenon occurs in various real-world systems like EEG-based brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), where input data distribution properties may shift over time. This paper introduces a covariate shift-detection and adaptation method for motor-imagery BCIs, employing an exponential weighted moving average (EWMA) model-based test on EEG signal features. The algorithm reconfigures the classifier's knowledge base, demonstrating effective covariate shift-detection and adaptation on the BCI Competition IV dataset 2A.'
