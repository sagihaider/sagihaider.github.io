excerpt: '[BIBM'14] Creating robust brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) from electroencephalogram (EEG) data faces challenges due to non-stationary EEG signal characteristics. Covariate shifts, caused by factors like electrode changes or varying impedances, can deteriorate BCI performance. This paper introduces a transductive learning algorithm to adapt to covariate shifts, enhancing BCI system accuracy on the BCI competition 2008-Graz dataset B. Results indicate improved performance compared to traditional learning methods, affirming the utility of covariate-shift adaptation for adaptive BCI systems.'
