excerpt: '[PO'19] This study explores factors influencing physical activity (PA) levels in 12-month infants. Analyzing data from 141 mother-infant pairs, the research identifies associations with PA, revealing that higher activity is linked to factors like being male, larger infant size, healthy maternal blood pressure, full-term gestation, and a healthy diet. A multivariable model confirms these associations, emphasizing the impact of both gestational and postnatal environmental factors on infant PA levels. The findings provide insights for interventions promoting healthier lives for infants and understanding the determinants of their PA.'
