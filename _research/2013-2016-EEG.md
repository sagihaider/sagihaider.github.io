Covariate shift appears only in X-> Y problems, and the case where the conditional probability P(Y\|X) in training and testing remains same, but the input distribution P(X) changes between training and testing. 


* The CSA method has been implemented using various computational intelligence methods such as transductive learning, semi-supervised learning, and ensemble learning. These CSA methods have been tested on 6 toy datasets.  

* A covariate shift in EEG data may arise while transitioning from training to testing session.

* An inductive-transductive model is introduced to track and address the effect of covariate shift in EEG signals using CSD test.

* The proposed model does not make any a-priori assumption about the distribution of the upcoming test data, and update its classification decision boundary using passive and active learning methods

* The proposed methods are applied to the two publically available datasets BCI competition-IV 2A & 2B for the single-trial EEG classification.
